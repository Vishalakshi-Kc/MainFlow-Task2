Goa Trip Plan

Day 1: North Goa Beaches

Morning:

  • Arrival in Goa: Check into your hotel in North Goa and freshen up.
  • Calangute Beach: Start your day at Calangute Beach, one of the most popular beaches in Goa.
  • Water Sports: Indulge in water sports like parasailing, jet-skiing, and banana boat rides

Afternoon:

  • Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a beach shack or a nearby restaurant
  • Baga Beach: Head to Baga Beach, known for its vibrant atmosphere and beach activities.
  • Anjuna Beach: Visit Anjuna Beach, famous for its rocky shores and flea market (if it's Wednesday).

Evening:

  • Chapora Fort: Visit the historic Chapora Fort for a stunning sunset view.
  • Dinner: Dine at a beachside restaurant or shack, enjoying fresh seafood and Goan delicacies.

Day 2: Exploring Culture and Nature

Morning:

  • Old Goa: Visit the historic churches of Old Goa, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus and Se Cathedral.
  • St. Augustine Tower: Explore the ruins of this historic site for a glimpse into Goa's past.

Afternoon:

  • Lunch: Have lunch at a local restaurant in Old Goa.
  • Spice Plantation Tour: Take a guided tour of a spice plantation, where you can learn about various spices and enjoy a traditional Goan meal

Evening:

  • Dona Paula: Visit the scenic Dona Paula viewpoint.
  • Miramar Beach: Spend some time at Miramar Beach, relaxing and enjoying the sunset.
  • Dinner: Enjoy dinner at a popular restaurant in Panaji, the capital city of Goa.

Day 3: South Goa Beaches and Relaxation

Morning:

  • Drive to South Goa: Check out from your hotel in North Goa and drive to South Goa.
  • Colva Beach: Start your day at Colva Beach, known for its serene and less crowded environment.

Afternoon:

  • Lunch: Have lunch at a beach shack or a nearby restaurant.
  • Palolem Beach: Head to Palolem Beach, one of the most beautiful beaches in Goa. Enjoy activities like kayaking, swimming, or simply relaxing by the shore.

Evening:

  • Sunset at Agonda Beach: Visit Agonda Beach for a tranquil sunset experience.
  • Dinner: Dine at a beachfront restaurant or shack, savoring the local flavors and fresh seafood.
